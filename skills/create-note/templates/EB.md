## When to use EB

An Executive Brief is a one-page distillation of a deeper RE for an executive audience (founders, investors, stakeholders, board). It is shorter, sharper, and decision-oriented compared to a full RE.

- **EB** is a distillate of an existing RE or analysis. Always references a longer source.
- **RE** is the analysis itself.

If no underlying analysis exists yet, write the RE first, then derive the EB from it. Do not write a standalone EB without source material.

## Section structure

1. **Summary** - 3-5 sentences max. The reader who reads only this section gets the full thesis.
2. **The Product / Position in One Line** - the single positioning sentence.
3. **Why It Matters / Wedge** - the differentiator versus competitors or status quo, ideally as a table.
4. **Proof** - quantified evidence (deployment metrics, traction, technical state). Tables preferred.
5. **Target Buyer / Audience** - who this is for, one short paragraph.
6. **Recommended Next Moves** - numbered, 3-5 actions max.
7. **Source** - wikilink to the underlying RE plus any companion artifacts.

Cut anything that does not directly serve an executive decision.

## Writing rules

- **One page when printed.** If it does not fit, cut content, do not shrink type.
- **Tables over prose** for any comparison (competitors, tiers, options, metrics).
- **Lead with conclusions.** No methodology preamble. No "we examined..." openers.
- **Bold the verdict** in each section if there is one.
- **Numbers are mandatory.** "Production deployment with 4,231 entities" beats "production deployment at scale."
- **No hedging.** Executives do not need "likely", "probably", "could potentially". Verify or cut.
- **Cite the source.** The source link at the bottom is mandatory; this document is a distillate, not the analysis.

## What to always cut

- Methodology sections
- Long bulleted lists of "things considered"
- Implementation details (those belong in the source RE or in a SPC)
- Background context the reader already has
- Caveats and qualifications that do not change the recommendation
